Frequently Asked Questions

What is robotic urology surgery?

+

Robotic urology surgery is an advanced minimally invasive surgery employing the robotic systems to carry out high accuracy while performing complex urological surgeries. This surgery is done with minor cuts and is quicker, less painful, and has occurred few complications in comparison with conventional open surgeries.

What types of urological conditions can be treated with robotic urology?

+

You are advised to see an IVF expert if you and your partner are able to conceive in a span of a year or more. Women who are aged more than 35 years and have been unable to conceive in a span of 6 months are also advised to see an expert. Also, if you are suffering from underlying conditions such as PCOS, endometriosis, or male factors then it is a matter of time and you should consult an expert to increase your chances of conception.

What are the benefits of robotic urology surgery?

+

Robotic urology surgery is an important advantage such as-small incisions, less pain and blood loss, fast recovery time, greater precision, and accuracy, short hospital stays-reduced risk of complications, minimal scarring-fast return to normal activities.

How long does recovery take after robotic urology surgery?

+

Recovery times vary by procedure and individual health conditions; however, robotic urology improves recovery time enormously over traditional open surgeries: many patients return to very light activity after just a few days to a week and return to all normal activities, usually within a few weeks.

Is robotic urology surgery safe?

+

Definitely yes. Robotic urology surgeries are absolutely safe, and these robotic systems are operated by expert surgeons who ensure that the surgery is done with perfection. Because of surgery being minimally invasive robotic urology, the risk of complication is reduced and the surgery as a whole is safer.

What can I expect during my robotic urology surgery consultation?

+

An experienced urologist is involved in this consultation who will assess your condition and what treatment options will be available, including the possibility of considering robotic urology. He or she will explain the procedure itself in detail, answer any questions, and personalize your care.

Do I need to stay in the hospital after robotic urology surgery?

+

Most treatments under robotic urology will require the patient to stay in the hospital for a very short time, one day to three days, depending on what is done and how the recovery is progressing. Your urologist will definitely get you specific instructions to follow based on the surgery and your condition.
